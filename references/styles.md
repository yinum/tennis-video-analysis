# Play-style archetypes and historical/pro comparison

Classify the player into ONE primary archetype (optionally a secondary tendency),
then support it with observable evidence: court position (Tier-2
`mean_dist_behind_baseline_m`, `net_approach_frac`, heatmaps), shot selection
patterns, and rally behavior. Style is about decisions and habits, not quality —
a 3.0 counterpuncher and a 5.5 counterpuncher share an identity.

## Archetypes

| Archetype | Observable signature | Historical exemplars |
|---|---|---|
| **Aggressive baseliner** | camps on/inside baseline, takes ball early, hunts forehands, direction changes to finish points, more winners than pushes | Sinner, Sabalenka, Agassi, Serena Williams |
| **Heavy-topspin grinder-aggressor** | high net clearance with dip, works points over many balls then strikes, extreme grips, big lateral defense | Nadal, Świątek, Alcaraz's rally mode |
| **Counterpuncher / defensive baseliner** | deep court position (2m+ behind baseline), exceptional retrieval, redirects pace rather than creating it, wins on error tempo | Medvedev, Murray, Wozniacki, Schwartzman |
| **All-court player** | mixes baseline and net, approaches behind quality, comfortable volleys, slice as a building tool | Federer, Alcaraz, Henin |
| **Serve-and-volley / net rusher** | serve+in patterns, chip-and-charge returns, high `net_approach_frac`, points end in ≤4 shots | Edberg, Sampras, Navratilova, McEnroe (historic); Tiafoe/Fritz sporadically (modern) |
| **Big server / first-strike** | free points on serve, serve+1 forehand identity, shorter rallies by design, tie-break oriented | Isner, Opelka, Raonic, Karlović |
| **Junkballer / disruptor** | pace-less mixes, slices, moonballs, drop shots, angle hunting; wins by discomfort | Santoro, Hsieh Su-wei, Bublik's soft mode |
| **Pusher (rec-level identity)** | returns everything with margin, near-zero risk, no weapon by choice; extremely effective ≤3.5 | no pro equivalent — treat as developmental stage of counterpuncher |

## Rules for honest pro comparison

- Frame as **pattern resemblance, not level**: "your court position and redirect
  habits resemble Medvedev's identity" — never "you play like Medvedev".
- Pick 1–3 pros whose *decision patterns* match; add one sentence each on what the
  player can copy from that pro next (a watchable homework assignment).
- Rec-level tells often override aesthetics: a player with a Federer-looking
  forehand who stands 3m behind the baseline and never approaches is a
  counterpuncher, not an all-courter.
- Doubles-heavy players skew toward net styles; note the session context.

## Specialties

List 2–4 concrete, evidence-backed specialties ("kick serve wide on ad side",
"backhand down-the-line under pressure", "drop-shot disguise"). A specialty is
something the player already does at a level above their overall band — these are
the assets the training plan builds around.
